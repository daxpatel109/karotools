<h2 id="what-is-a-gst-invoice">What is a GST Invoice?</h2>

A **Tax Invoice** (commonly called a GST Invoice) is a legally binding document issued by a registered dealer to the purchaser, showing the details of goods/services supplied and the taxes charged. Under the CGST Act, 2017, issuing a compliant tax invoice is mandatory for registered businesses to pass on the Input Tax Credit (ITC) to the buyer.

<h2 id="what-should-it-include">What should a GST invoice format in Excel include?</h2>

Issuing a proper GST bill format is not just about professionalism; it's a strict legal requirement in India. If your invoice lacks specific mandated details, your buyer cannot claim Input Tax Credit (ITC) for the tax they paid to you. This could severely hurt your business relationships. For freelancers and small businesses who just completed their [GST registration](/guides/how-to-register-gst-online), setting up the perfect format is your absolute first priority before billing any client.

<h2 id="mandatory-fields">required fields in a GST Invoice</h2>

According to Rule 46 of the CGST Rules, to be legally valid and allow your client to claim ITC, your invoice must include 16 specific details. When setting up an **excel gst invoice format**, ensure these **gst invoice required fields** are clearly present:

1. **Tax Invoice Header:** The document must clearly state "Tax Invoice" at the very top.
2. **Supplier Details:** Your registered business name, exact registered address, and your 15-digit GSTIN.
3. **Invoice Number:** A unique, consecutive serial number (containing alphabets, numerals, or special characters like hyphen/slash) not exceeding 16 characters for a single financial year.
4. **Date of Issue:** The exact date the invoice is created and issued to the client.
5. **Buyer Details (Billed To):** Name, address, and GSTIN (if they are a registered business) of the recipient.
6. **Delivery Details (Shipped To):** Delivery address, if it differs from the billing address (crucial for physical goods).
7. **Place of Supply:** The state name and state code where the supply is consumed. This determines whether to apply IGST or CGST/SGST.
8. **HSN or SAC Code:** Harmonized System of Nomenclature (for goods) or Services Accounting Code (for services).
9. **Item Description:** A clear, unambiguous description of the goods or services provided.
10. **Quantity & Unit:** Quantity, unit (e.g., pieces, kilograms, hours), and the base rate per unit.
11. **Taxable Value:** The total value of goods/services before any tax or discount is applied.
12. **Tax Rate:** The exact rate of tax (e.g., 5%, 12%, 18%) applied to that specific HSN/SAC.
13. **Tax Amount Breakdown:** The calculated tax amount explicitly broken down into Central Tax (CGST), State Tax (SGST), or Integrated Tax (IGST).
14. **Total Amount:** The final grand total of the invoice, combining the taxable value and all taxes.
15. **Reverse Charge:** A clear indication of whether tax is payable on a reverse charge basis (usually "No" for standard invoices).
16. **Signature:** A physical signature, or a valid digital signature of the supplier or authorized representative.

<h2 id="invoice-types">GST Invoice vs. Regular Invoice vs. Bill of Supply</h2>

It is critical to understand the distinction between document types under the GST regime:

- **Regular Invoice / Proforma Invoice:** A proforma invoice is a draft or estimate sent before work is completed. It is not a legal tax document and cannot be used to claim ITC.
- **Tax Invoice (GST Invoice):** This is the strict legal document discussed in this guide. It contains the explicit breakdown of CGST/SGST/IGST and allows the buyer to claim Input Tax Credit.
- **Bill of Supply:** Issued by businesses registered under the [Composition Scheme](/blog/gst-composition-scheme-limits) or by businesses selling entirely GST-exempt goods. A Bill of Supply does not charge any GST, and the buyer cannot claim any ITC.

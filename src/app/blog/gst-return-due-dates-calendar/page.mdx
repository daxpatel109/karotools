## What Are GST Returns?

A GST return is a document containing details of all the income (sales) and expenses (purchases) that a taxpayer must file with the Indian tax authorities. ## Quarterly Returns: QRMP Scheme Due Dates

The Quarterly Return Filing and Monthly Payment of Taxes (QRMP) scheme is designed for small businesses and freelancers with an annual aggregate turnover of up to ₹5 Crores. Under this scheme, you file returns quarterly but must pay your estimated tax monthly using form PMT-06.

## CMP-08 & GSTR-4 Due Dates for Composition Dealers

If you have opted for the [GST Composition Scheme](/blog/gst-composition-scheme-limits) to reduce your compliance burden, you do not file the standard GSTR-1 or GSTR-3B. Instead, you file a simple statement called Form CMP-08 every quarter, and an annual return called GSTR-4.

- **CMP-08 Due Date:** 18th of the month succeeding the quarter. (e.g., For April-June quarter, it is due by 18th July).
- **GSTR-4 Due Date:** 30th April following the end of the financial year.

## GST Late Fee for Delayed Returns

If you miss your **GST return due dates**, you will be automatically penalized by the GST portal. The standard **GST late fee for return filing** under the CGST Act is:

- **Standard Return:** ₹50 per day (₹25 CGST + ₹25 SGST) of delay. 
- **Nil Return:** ₹20 per day (₹10 CGST + ₹10 SGST) of delay. A "Nil return" means you had absolutely no sales or purchases in that billing period.

*Maximum Cap:* To provide relief to small taxpayers, the maximum late fee is capped at ₹2,000 for standard returns and ₹500 for Nil returns per filing period.

## GST Interest on Late Tax Payment

Aside from the flat daily late fee, if you owe the government tax money and pay it *after* the due date, you must pay interest on the outstanding liability.
- The statutory interest rate is **18% per annum**.
- It is calculated on the net tax liability (Tax Owed minus available Input Tax Credit).
- Interest is calculated from the day following the due date until the actual date of payment.

### Example Late Fee & Interest Calculation

Imagine you are a freelancer who missed filing your May 2026 GSTR-3B. The due date was 20th June 2026. You finally file it on 30th June 2026 (a delay of 10 days). You owe ₹10,000 in net tax.

- **Late Fee:** 10 days x ₹50/day = ₹500.
- **Interest:** ₹10,000 x 18% x (10 days / 365 days) = ₹49.31.
- **Total Extra Penalty:** You will pay your ₹10,000 tax plus ₹549.31 in penalties.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the GSTR-1 due date?**  
For monthly filers, the GSTR-1 due date is the 11th of the following month. For quarterly filers (QRMP scheme), it is the 13th of the month following the end of the quarter.

**What is the GSTR-3B due date?**  
For monthly filers, the GSTR-3B due date is the 20th of the following month. For quarterly filers, it is the 22nd or 24th of the month following the quarter, depending on your state.

**What is the due date for GST return filing in 2026?**  
There are multiple due dates depending on your return type. Generally, monthly sales (GSTR-1) are due on the 11th, and monthly tax payments (GSTR-3B) are due on the 20th of every month.

**What is the late fee for delayed GST return filing?**  
The late fee is typically ₹50 per day of delay. If it is a Nil return (no business activity), the late fee is reduced to ₹20 per day.

**What is the difference between GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B?**  
GSTR-1 is a statement of all your outward supplies (sales), showing the invoices you issued. GSTR-3B is a summary return where you declare your total tax liability, claim Input Tax Credit (ITC), and actually pay the tax.

**Who files CMP-08?**  
CMP-08 is filed by businesses registered under the GST Composition Scheme. It is a quarterly statement-cum-challan to declare details or pay tax.

**What happens if I miss the GST return due date?**  
If you miss the due date, you will incur a daily late fee. Additionally, if you have outstanding tax to pay, you will be charged 18% per annum interest on the tax amount.

**How can I calculate GST late fee?**  
You can calculate your exact penalty by multiplying the number of days delayed by ₹50 (or ₹20 for Nil returns) and adding 18% interest on the unpaid tax. You can also use the free KaroTools GST Late Fee Calculator to do the math automatically.

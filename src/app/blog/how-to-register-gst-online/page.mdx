Registering for GST is mandatory for many businesses and freelancers in India. This step-by-step guide walks you through the entire online registration process on the official portal.

## 1. Before You Start: Documents Needed

Before you open the portal, gather digital copies of your documents. Having everything ready ensures your session won't time out.

- PAN Card of the business/proprietor
- Aadhaar Card
- Proof of Business Address (Rent agreement, electricity bill, or NOC)
- Bank Account Proof (Cancelled cheque or passbook extract)
- Photograph of the proprietor/directors

## 2. Step 1: Visit GST Registration Portal

First, navigate to the official Indian government GST portal.

- Open your browser and go to [https://www.gst.gov.in](https://www.gst.gov.in)
- Click on **Services** > **Registration** > **New Registration**.

## 3. Step 2: Generate TRN

You must first generate a Temporary Reference Number (TRN).

- Select "New Registration".
- Choose your taxpayer type (usually "Taxpayer").
- Select your State and District.
- Enter your Legal Name (exactly as on PAN) and your PAN number.
- Enter your active Email ID and Mobile Number.

## 4. Step 3: Verify OTP

After clicking proceed:
- You will receive two separate OTPs (one on email, one on mobile).
- Enter both OTPs to verify your contact details.
- The system will instantly generate your **TRN**.

## 5. Step 4: Fill Part B

Log back in using your TRN and the new OTP. You now need to complete "Part B" of the application, which has several tabs.

- **Business Details:** Trade name, constitution of business.
- **Promoters/Partners:** Personal details, DIN, Aadhaar.
- **Authorized Signatory:** Designate who will sign returns.
- **Principal Place of Business:** Enter your exact address.

## 6. Step 5: Upload Documents

In the respective tabs of Part B, you must upload your proof documents. Ensure files are within the maximum size limits (usually 100KB for photos, 1MB for PDFs). Upload your address proof and bank details carefully.

## 7. Step 6: Submit Application

After filling all tabs:
- Go to the **Verification** tab.
- Check the declaration box.
- Submit the application using **DSC** (Digital Signature Certificate) or **EVC** (Electronic Verification Code via Aadhaar OTP).
- You will receive an **Application Reference Number (ARN)** on your email to track your status.

## 8. Common GST Registration Rejection Reasons

If your application is rejected, it is usually due to:
- Mismatch between PAN name and Trade name.
- Blurry or illegible document uploads.
- Rent agreement not stamped or expired.
- NOC from the landlord missing for shared/residential addresses.

## 10. FAQ

**How long does it take to get a GST number?**
If you opt for Aadhaar authentication and all documents are correct, you usually receive your GSTIN within 3 to 7 working days.

**Is there a government fee for GST registration?**
No, GST registration on the official portal is 100% free.

**Can I register for GST using my residential address?**
Yes, freelancers and small business owners can use their home address. You will need to provide an electricity bill and a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the owner (if the house is not in your name).

## What is a GST Invoice?

A GST invoice (or tax invoice) is a legal document issued by a registered seller to a buyer. It itemizes the goods or services provided, their quantities, and the total amount due, while explicitly breaking down the taxes charged under the Goods and Services Tax (GST) system—namely CGST, SGST, and IGST. 

Issuing a proper GST bill format is not just about professionalism; it's a legal requirement. If your invoice lacks specific details, your buyer cannot claim Input Tax Credit (ITC), which could hurt your business relationships. For freelancers and small businesses who just completed [GST registration](/blog/how-to-register-gst-online), getting this format right is your first priority.

## Mandatory Fields in a GST Invoice

According to Rule 46 of the CGST Rules, to be legally valid, your invoice must include certain details. When setting up an **excel gst invoice format**, ensure these **gst invoice mandatory fields** are present:

1. **Supplier Details:** Your business name, address, and GSTIN.
2. **Tax Invoice Header:** The document must clearly state "Tax Invoice" at the top.
3. **Invoice Number:** A unique, consecutive serial number (containing alphabets, numerals, or special characters like hyphen/slash) not exceeding 16 characters for a financial year.
4. **Date of Issue:** The date the invoice is created.
5. **Buyer Details:** Name, address, and GSTIN (if registered) of the recipient.
6. **Place of Supply:** The state name and code, crucial for determining whether to apply IGST or CGST/SGST.
7. **HSN or SAC Code:** Harmonized System of Nomenclature (for goods) or Services Accounting Code (for services).
8. **Item Description:** Clear description of the goods or services.
9. **Quantity & Taxable Value:** Quantity, unit, and the value of goods/services before tax.
10. **Tax Rate & Amount:** The exact rate of tax (e.g., 5%, 12%, 18%) and the tax amount broken down into CGST, SGST, or IGST.
11. **Signature:** A physical or digital signature of the supplier or authorized representative.

## GST Invoice vs. Regular Invoice

A regular invoice simply requests payment for goods or services and lists a total amount. A **GST Invoice**, however, is a strictly regulated document. 

The primary difference is the explicit inclusion of the GSTINs of both parties, the HSN/SAC codes, and the segregated breakdown of taxes (CGST, SGST, IGST). A regular invoice (or a "Bill of Supply" issued by businesses under the [Composition Scheme](/blog/gst-composition-scheme-limits)) does not allow the buyer to claim ITC.
